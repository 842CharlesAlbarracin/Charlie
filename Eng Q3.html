What is Bias?

Bias is prejudice toward someone, something, somewhere, or a side of a story. A author has bias if they do not show the sides equally and only defends one side for one's benefit of belief.

How to Identify Bias?

  • If it focuses only one side of the story.
  • If the author defends only one side.
  • If the author exaggerated a action of one side.
  • If the author tries to show opinion as a fact.
  • If there is a political slant.
  • If they backup their evidence with false statements.
  • If they are using a strong language for or against a side of the story.
  • And Etc.

Types of Poetry

  • Lyric- Used in kinds of songs to express one's self and to show a topic and to deliver the topic in a way of song to interest people to hearing it.
  • Narrative- A form of poetry in a form of a story to interest the reader and to show a topic in that form.

Types of Prose

  • Fiction- A form of literature that says things about a topic in a fiction world or format to interest the reader in things like a short story, or a novel.
  • Non-Fiction- A form of literature in a essay to tackle a topic and to being straight to the point and base it on facts. To make it easier for those who like to know it straight to the point.
