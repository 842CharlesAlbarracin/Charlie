What is The Cell Cycle and Genetics?

  • The Cell Cycle is how more cells are made. And Genetics is how much they are similar to the parent compared to the child's genetics and how they combined.
  1. The Two Types.
    • Mitosis-Cells for the Body - 2 Daughter Cells - Maintains Chromosome Numbers
    • Melosis-Cells for Reproduction - 4 Daughter Cells - Lowers Chromosome Numbers

Sequence

  • Parent cell-Daughter cell-Tissue-Organ-Organ System

Phases for Mitosis

  1. Prophase
    • The condensation of chromosomes, the movement of the centrosomes, the formation of the mitotic spindle, and the beginning of nucleoli break down.
  2. Metaphase
    • The nucleus dissolves and the cell's chromosomes condense and move together, aligning in the center of the dividing cell.
  3. Anaphase
    • Separates the duplicated genetic material carried in the nucleus of a parent cell into two identical daughter cells.
  4. Telophase
    • separates the duplicated genetic material carried in the nucleus of a parent cell into two identical daughter cells.
